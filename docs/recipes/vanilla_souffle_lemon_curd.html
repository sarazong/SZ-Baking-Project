Vanilla Souffle with Lemon Curd

Published

November 21, 2025

Modified

December 9, 2025

Ingredients

  • Vanilla Souffles (amount: four 8oz ramekins)
  • Lemon Curd (amount: ~120 ml)
Quantity
1. large eggs (50g each without shell) 4
2. sugar for egg yolk 15 g
3. vanilla extract 15 ml1
4. cake flour 25 g2
5. milk 200 ml
6. unsalted butter 35 g
7. sugar for egg white 40 g3
8. powder sugar 1 Tbsp
1 Original recipe uses 4g of vanilla bean paste and it's equivalent to 1 Tbsp of vanilla extract according to websearch.
2 Can use 3g corn starch plus 22g all purpose flour as the cake flour substitute.
3 Original recipe uses 55g of sugar for egg white, reduce to 40g since enjoying the souffle with some lemon curd.
Quantity1
1. large eggs (50g each without shell) 1
2. lemon zest 1 lemon
3. lemon juice (freshly squeezed) 40 g
4. butter (unsalted/lightly salted) 18 g
5. sugar 28 g
6. sea salt (fine) 1/24 tsp
1 Original recipe uses 6 eggs, 6-8 lemons, 240g lemon juice, 113g unsalted butter, 200g sugar, and 1/4 tsp fine sea salt.

High-level How To

  • Souffle
  • Lemon Curd
  1. Grease ramekins with butter and coat with sugar.
  2. Whisk the egg yolks with the sugar.
  3. Sift in the flour to step 2, mix well.
  4. Warm up the milk and butter to ~40C and pour in 20 ml to step 3, mix well.
  5. Add the rest of the milk, cook on low heat and mix constantly until thicken to paste.
  6. Transfer to a bowl, add the vanilla extract, mix well.
  7. Cover the custard with a plastic wrap and cool to room temperature.
  8. Whisk the egg white with a hand mixer and add sugar 1/3 at a time, until soft peaks.
  9. Fold in the meringue to the custard 1/3 at a time with a whisk and a spatula.
  10. Divide the batter among the ramekins.
  11. Bake at 400F for about 15mins until the top is brown.
  12. Dust with powdered sugar immediately after taking out.
  1. Rub sugar and lemon zest together in a pot until like wet sand.
  2. Add lemon juice to step 1.
  3. Whisk egg with salt, add to step 2, and mix well.
  4. Cook on low heat, and mix constantly until the mix thicken to sauce.
  5. Sift mix to butter.

Sara Z’s Notes/Tips:

Vanilla souffle:

  1. To coat the ramekins with butter and sugar, first melt a small amount of butter in a microwave or in some hot water. Brush the butter onto the ramekin with upward strokes to help the souffle batter rise vertically when bake. Put a table spoon of granulated sugar in the ramekin and rotate it until its inside is fully coated with sugar. Coating the side of the ramekin with sugar provides surface for the souffle batter to rise.
  2. When cooking the flour-milk mix in step 5 into paste, make sure to constantly stir the mix on low heat to ensure even cooking and prevent burn. Turn off the stove when the mix has similar consistency as the soft peak meringue. Transfer the mix into a bowl to prevent further cooking in the hot pot.

Lemon curd:

  1. Zest the lemon(s), but don’t cut too deep to avoid the pith, which is the white, spongy part of the lemon peel that could taste bitter.
